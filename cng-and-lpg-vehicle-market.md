<p><p>CNG (Compressed Natural Gas) and LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas) vehicles are alternative fuel vehicles that have gained popularity in recent years due to their lower greenhouse gas emissions and cost-effectiveness compared to traditional gasoline and diesel vehicles.</p><p>CNG vehicles use natural gas, which is mainly composed of methane, as fuel. The natural gas is compressed and stored in high-pressure tanks, which are then used to power the engine. LPG vehicles, on the other hand, use propane or butane, which are byproducts of petroleum refining, as fuel. LPG is stored in liquid form in pressurized tanks and is converted into gas before being fed into the engine.</p><p>The global CNG and LPG vehicle market have witnessed significant growth in recent years, primarily driven by strict government regulations to reduce vehicular emissions and the increasing adoption of alternative fuel vehicles by commercial and industrial sectors. Additionally, rising environmental concerns and growing consumer awareness regarding the advantages of CNG and LPG vehicles have further boosted market growth.</p><p>The market analysis suggests that the Asia-Pacific region dominates the global CNG and LPG vehicle market due to the presence of developing economies like India and China, which have implemented several initiatives to promote the adoption of alternative fuel vehicles.
