<p><p>The femoral hip stems market consists of three types: cemented, cementless, and hybrid. Cemented femoral hip stems involve the use of bone cement for fixation. Cementless femoral hip stems rely on bone ingrowth for stability and are typically coated with a porous surface. Hybrid femoral hip stems combine the use of cement and bone ingrowth for fixation. Each type caters to different patient needs and surgeon preferences, offering various advantages and considerations in terms of implant stability and long-term outcomes.</p></p>
