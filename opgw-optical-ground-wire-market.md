<p><p>OPGW (Optical Ground Wire) is a type of fiber optic cable that is used in power transmission and distribution networks. It is added to overhead power lines to provide both electrical grounding and fiber optic communication capabilities. OPGW generally consists of optical fibers surrounded by aluminum or stainless steel tubes, which provide mechanical strength and protect the fibers from environmental factors.</p><p>The OPGW market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The advantages offered by OPGW, such as high bandwidth capacity, low transmission loss, and resistance to electromagnetic interference, are also contributing to its increasing adoption.</p><p>Moreover, the deployment of renewable energy sources like wind and solar power is further fueling the demand for OPGW. <p><p>OPGW (Optical Ground Wire) is a type of cable used by electrical utilities, which combines optical fibers for communication purposes with an electrical conductor. There are two main market types of OPGW. Central Tube Structure OPGW has optical fibers placed in a central tube, surrounded by strength members. Layer Stranding Structure OPGW has multiple layers of optical fibers and strength members, providing better protection against mechanical stresses. Both types offer efficient and reliable communication and can be used in various applications within the electrical utility industry.</p></p>
