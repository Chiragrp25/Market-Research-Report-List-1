<p><p>Mechanical dewatering equipment refers to various types of machinery used to remove water from solids. Sieve dehydration involves passing the mixture through a mesh or sieve, allowing water to drain while retaining the solids. Centrifugal dehydration utilizes centrifugal force to separate the liquid and solid phases. Filtration dehydration employs a porous material to trap solid particles while allowing water to pass through. These methods are commonly used across industries to reduce water content in various materials, such as sludge, wastewater, and food processing byproducts.</p></p>
