<p><p>The microbial inoculants market encompasses three major types: bacterials, fungal inoculants, and composite inoculants. Bacterial inoculants consist of beneficial bacteria strains that enhance the soil's nutrient availability and plant growth. Fungal inoculants, on the other hand, introduce beneficial fungi to the soil, promoting nutrient absorption and disease resistance in plants. Lastly, composite inoculants combine both bacteria and fungi, providing a holistic approach to improving soil health and plant productivity. These microbial inoculants are crucial in sustainable agriculture practices, supporting efficient nutrient uptake and reducing reliance on synthetic fertilizers.</p></p>
