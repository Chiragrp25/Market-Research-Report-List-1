<p><p>Soft Magnetic Composites (SMCs) are novel materials used in various applications for their soft magnetic properties. The market for SMCs can be subdivided into different types, namely soft ferrite, electrical steel, and others. Soft ferrite SMCs possess high resistivity and are primarily used in high-frequency applications, such as telecommunications and power electronics. Electrical steel SMCs exhibit low hysteresis loss and are widely utilized in motors, generators, and transformers. The "others" category includes SMCs with unique properties and applications, like amorphous metal cores used in sensors and actuators, making them versatile options in the electromagnetic devices industry.</p></p>
