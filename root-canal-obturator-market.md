<p><p>A root canal obturator is a dental instrument used in endodontic procedures to fill and seal the root canal space after the removal of infected or damaged pulp. It is a tapered, rubber-like material that is inserted into the canal to prevent reinfection and restore the tooth's function. Root canal obturators are typically made of gutta percha, a natural polymer derived from the sap of certain trees.</p><p>The root canal obturator market is expected to experience substantial growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of dental diseases such as dental caries and gum diseases is a major driver for market growth. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, which is more prone to dental issues, is expected to contribute to market expansion.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing awareness of oral health among the general population, leading to an increased demand for dental treatments. Technological advancements in endodontic procedures and materials are also driving market growth. For instance, the introduction of thermoplastic gutta percha obturators has improved the efficiency and precision of root canal treatments.</p><p>Geographically, North America is the largest market for root canal obturators due to the high prevalence of dental diseases and the presence of a well-established healthcare infrastructure.
