<p><p>A negative photoresist stripper is a chemical solution used in the microelectronics industry to remove negative photoresist films from substrates. Negative photoresist is a photosensitive material used in the photolithography process, which is integral to manufacturing electronic components such as integrated circuits and printed circuit boards. After the exposure and development process, the negative photoresist stripper is applied to dissolve and remove the unwanted areas of the photoresist film, leaving only the desired pattern on the substrate.</p><p>The negative photoresist stripper market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The negative photoresist stripper market comprises three main types: negative etch resists, thick negative resists, and negative lift-off resists. Negative etch resists are used for etching processes in semiconductor manufacturing, where the resist material protects certain areas during etching. Thick negative resists help create deeper features and patterned structures. Lastly, negative lift-off resists enable the selective removal of a resist layer, allowing the deposition of new materials in desired areas during device fabrication. These different types cater to diverse applications in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
