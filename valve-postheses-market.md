<p><p>The valve prostheses market is divided into three main types: transcatheter valves, tissue valves, and mechanical valves. Transcatheter valves are implanted using minimally invasive procedures, avoiding open-heart surgery. Tissue valves are made from animal tissues and provide better hemodynamics, but have a limited lifespan. Mechanical valves, made from synthetic materials, are durable but require long-term anticoagulant therapy. Each type offers unique benefits and drawbacks, catering to specific patient needs. The market for valve prostheses is thus diverse, with various options available to patients and healthcare providers.</p></p>
