The MIBK market can be classified into two types: the Isopropanol Method and the Acetone Method. In the Isopropanol Method, MIBK is produced by reacting isopropanol with acetone. This method offers advantages such as higher purity and higher yield. On the other hand, the Acetone Method uses a direct oxidation process of acetone, which requires lower energy consumption but may result in lower purity and yield of MIBK. Both methods cater to different needs and preferences in the market.</p></p>
