<p><p>The rubber repair glue market consists of two main types: hot bond and cold bond. Hot bond glue refers to adhesives that require the application of heat during the bonding process. This is typically achieved using specialized equipment such as hot presses or vulcanizing machines. On the other hand, cold bond glue refers to adhesives that do not require heat and can be used at room temperature. These adhesives often rely on chemical reactions to create a strong bond between rubber surfaces. Both types of glue serve the purpose of repairing rubber materials.</p></p>
