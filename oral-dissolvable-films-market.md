<p><p>The oral dissolvable films market consists of two types: oral dispersible films and sublingual films. Oral dispersible films are thin strips that can be easily dissolved in the mouth without the need for water. These films are commonly used for delivering medication to patients who have difficulty swallowing pills or tablets. On the other hand, sublingual films are placed under the tongue, where they rapidly dissolve and release the drug, allowing for quick absorption into the bloodstream. Both types provide a convenient and effective alternative to traditional dosage forms.</p></p>
