<p><p>Hydrogen atomic clocks are vital devices used for measuring time with exceptional accuracy. The market consists of two main types: passive type and active type. </p><p>Passive type hydrogen atomic clocks rely on natural decay of hydrogen atoms to measure time. They do not require external energy sources or intervention, making them highly reliable and self-sustainable. These clocks are widely used in critical applications such as deep space navigation and satellite communications.</p><p>On the other hand, active type hydrogen atomic clocks maintain precise timing through continuous manipulation of hydrogen atoms. They employ techniques like laser cooling and trapping to enhance accuracy. These clocks are commonly used in scientific research, metrology laboratories, and high-precision navigation systems.</p></p>
