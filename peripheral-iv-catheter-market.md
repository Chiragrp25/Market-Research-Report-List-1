<p><p>The Peripheral I.V. Catheter (PIVC) market consists of two main types: Short PIVC and Integrated/Closed PIVC. Short PIVC refers to a catheter that is inserted into a vein near the surface of the skin and is typically used for short-term medical treatments, such as administering medications or fluids. On the other hand, Integrated/Closed PIVC is a more advanced type that includes additional features like integrated needle shielding and closed systems to reduce the risk of infections and needlestick injuries. This type is commonly used in hospitals and clinics for long-term treatments.</p></p>
