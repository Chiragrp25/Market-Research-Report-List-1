<p><p>The nanofiltration membranes market can be categorized into three types: polymeric, ceramic, and hybrid. Polymeric membranes are made from high-performance polymers and are widely used for water and wastewater treatment. Ceramic membranes, on the other hand, are made from inorganic materials such as metal oxides and offer higher chemical and thermal stability, making them suitable for harsh operating conditions. Hybrid membranes combine the properties of both polymeric and ceramic membranes, providing enhanced separation performance and durability, making them suitable for a wide range of applications.</p></p>
