<p><p>Positive Airway Pressure (PAP) interface is a medical device used in the treatment of sleep apnea, a condition in which the airway is partially or completely blocked during sleep, leading to interrupted breathing. PAP interface devices, such as masks and nasal pillows, deliver pressurized air to the patient's airway, maintaining an open and continuous flow of oxygen and preventing airway collapse.</p><p>The positive airway pressure interface market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The market is driven by the increasing prevalence of sleep apnea, growing awareness about the benefits of positive airway pressure therapy, and advancements in PAP interface devices.</p><p>According to market analysis, the global positive airway pressure interface market was valued at approximately $2.4 billion in 2020 and is projected to reach $3.4 billion by 2027, growing at a CAGR of 4.2% during the forecast period. <p><p>Positive airway pressure interface market types include face masks, nasal masks, oral masks, and helmets. Face masks are worn over the nose and mouth, while nasal masks cover the nose only. Oral masks, on the other hand, cover the mouth and may be suitable for individuals who cannot tolerate nasal masks. Helmets are a less common option that covers the entire head. These different types of interfaces allow individuals with sleep apnea or other breathing disorders to receive continuous positive airway pressure therapy, ensuring the airway remains open during sleep.</p></p>
