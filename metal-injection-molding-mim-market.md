<p><p>Metal Injection Molding (MIM) is a manufacturing process that combines metal powder and a binder material to create complex-shaped metal components. The MIM market can be divided into three types based on the methods used to produce metal powder: the Carbonyl Method, High-Pressure Gas Atomization, and Ultra-High Pressure Water Atomization. The Carbonyl Method involves the decomposition of metal carbonyls to obtain metal powder, while High-Pressure Gas Atomization employs high-pressure gas jets to atomize molten metal. Ultra-High Pressure Water Atomization uses water jets at extremely high pressures to break up molten metal into powder form.</p></p>
