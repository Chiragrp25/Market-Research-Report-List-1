<p><p>Transcranial Dopplers (TCD) are medical devices used to assess blood flow in the brain. The TCD market includes two types: implantable TCD and functional TCD (fTCD). Implantable TCDs are surgically implanted devices that continuously monitor blood flow in real-time. They are typically used in critical care settings to detect brain injuries or monitor patients with high risk of stroke. On the other hand, fTCDs are non-invasive devices that measure blood flow changes during brain activity, helping researchers understand brain function and diagnose disorders like dyslexia or autism.</p></p>
