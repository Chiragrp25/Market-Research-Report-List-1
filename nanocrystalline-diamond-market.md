<p><p>The Nanocrystalline Diamond market can be classified into two types: HPHT (High Pressure, High Temperature) and CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition). In the HPHT method, diamonds are created under extreme pressure and temperature conditions, mimicking the natural diamond formation process. This method produces high-quality diamonds that are often used in industrial applications. On the other hand, CVD involves the deposition of carbon atoms onto a substrate, resulting in the formation of synthetic diamonds. These diamonds are widely used in electronics, cutting tools, and jewelry due to their excellent thermal and electrical conductivity.</p></p>
