<p><p>The point-of-care breathalyzer market consists of three main types: desktop, portable, and handheld. Desktop breathalyzers are larger, stationary devices typically found in clinical settings. They offer advanced features and accuracy but lack portability. Portable breathalyzers are smaller, battery-operated devices that are easy to carry. They provide accurate results and can be used both in clinical and non-clinical settings. Handheld breathalyzers are compact and lightweight, allowing convenient on-the-go testing. While they may sacrifice some accuracy for convenience, they are suitable for personal use or law enforcement purposes.</p></p>
