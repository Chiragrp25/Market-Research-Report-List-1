<p><p>Rheology modifiers are substances used in cosmetics to modify the flow characteristics and consistency of products, such as lotions, creams, and gels. There are two main types: aqueous rheology modifiers and non-aqueous rheology modifiers. Aqueous rheology modifiers are used in water-based products, like shampoos, and are designed to enhance stability, thickness, and texture. Non-aqueous rheology modifiers, on the other hand, are used in oil-based products, like lipsticks and foundations, and are aimed at improving spreadability, thickness, and suspension of particles. Both types play a crucial role in achieving the desired performance and sensory attributes of cosmetic products.</p></p>
