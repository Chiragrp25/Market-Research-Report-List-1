<p><p>Dimethylaminoethyl acrylate (DMAEA) is a chemical compound used in various industries. It is primarily produced through three main methods: direct esterification, acryl chloride method, and transesterification. In the direct esterification market, DMAEA is synthesized by reacting acrylate with dimethylaminoethanol. The acryl chloride method involves the reaction of acryloyl chloride with dimethylaminoethanol. Lastly, in the transesterification market, DMAEA is produced by reacting dimethylaminoethyl methacrylate with methanol. These different production methods enable DMAEA to be used in a range of applications like adhesives, coatings, and textile treatments.</p></p>
