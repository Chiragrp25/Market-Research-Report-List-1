<p><p>Ethylene-propylene elastomers, also known as EP elastomers or EPDM rubber, are synthetic elastomers widely used in various industries due to their excellent resistance to weathering, ozone, heat, and chemicals. The increasing demand for EP elastomers in the automotive industry, particularly for vehicle seals and gaskets, is a major driver of market growth. EP elastomers are preferred in the automotive sector due to their high thermal stability, low gas permeability, and good compression set resistance.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing construction and infrastructure activities fuel the demand for EP elastomers in roofing membranes, sealants, and insulation materials. The superior weatherability and durability of EP elastomers make them suitable for various construction applications.</p><p>In terms of regional analysis, Asia-Pacific is projected to dominate the ethylene-propylene elastomers market during the forecast period. <p><p>Ethylene-Propylene Elastomers (EPM/EPDM) are synthetic rubber materials used in various industries. In terms of market types, there are two main categories: Compatible and Miscible. Compatible elastomers can be blended with other rubber materials without causing phase separation, allowing for better processability and customization. On the other hand, miscible elastomers can fully mix with other rubber materials at a molecular level, resulting in improved physical properties and uniformity. These market types provide different options for manufacturers depending on the desired properties and applications of the end product.</p></p>
