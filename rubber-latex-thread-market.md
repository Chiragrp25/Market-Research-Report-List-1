<p><p>The rubber latex thread market can be categorized into three types based on gauge count. Coarse gauge, ranging from count 20# to 46#, is used for products requiring low elasticity, such as shoe laces. Medium gauge, count 48# to 80#, offers moderate elasticity and is commonly used for knitting. Fine gauge, with a count above 80#, possesses high elasticity and is preferred for making products like gloves and socks. These gauge types enable manufacturers to produce latex threads that suit specific product requirements in terms of elasticity.</p></p>
