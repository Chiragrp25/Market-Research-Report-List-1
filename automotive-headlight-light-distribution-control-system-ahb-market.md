<p><p>The Automotive Headlight Light Distribution Control System (AHB) is a technology that helps improve visibility and safety while driving at night. There are two main types of AHB systems: Adaptive Front-lighting System (AFS) and Adaptive Driving Beam System (ADB). AFS adjusts the direction and intensity of the headlights based on the vehicle's speed, steering angle, and road conditions to enhance visibility around bends. ADB, on the other hand, automatically switches between high and low beams to avoid dazzling oncoming drivers while providing optimum illumination for the road ahead.</p></p>
