Voltage followers use OTAs to provide high input impedance and low output impedance, ensuring accurate signal replication. OTAs are used in current-controlled amplifiers to regulate gain and dynamically control signal strength. In filters, OTAs play a crucial role in achieving precise frequency responses. OTAs also serve as multipliers, performing analog signal multiplication. Comparators utilize OTAs to compare voltage levels and trigger actions accordingly. Additionally, OTAs find applications in other diverse markets, showcasing their versatile nature.</p></p>
