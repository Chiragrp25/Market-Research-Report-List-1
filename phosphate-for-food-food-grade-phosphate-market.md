<p><p>Phosphate for food, also known as food-grade phosphate, refers to a group of chemical compounds used in the food industry for various purposes. Different types of food-grade phosphates include sodium tripolyphosphate (STPP), sodium hexametaphosphate (SHMP), sodium acid pyrophosphate (SAPP), and tetrasodium pyrophosphate (TSPP). STPP is utilized as a preservative in processed meats and seafood, while SHMP is used as a thickening and emulsifying agent. SAPP is commonly used as a leavening agent in bakery products, and TSPP is used as a buffering and chelating agent in foods and beverages.</p></p>
