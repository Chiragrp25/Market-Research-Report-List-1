<p><p>Bio herbicides are a type of eco-friendly herbicides derived from natural sources to control unwanted plants. The market can be categorized into three types: granular, liquid, and others. Granular bio herbicides refer to solid particles that can be spread on the soil surface, releasing the herbicidal components gradually. Liquid bio herbicides, on the other hand, are in liquid form and are sprayed directly onto the plants. The "others" category encompasses alternative forms such as powder or gel-based bio herbicides. All these types offer effective and sustainable solutions to manage weed growth while minimizing environmental impacts.</p></p>

<p><p>Bio herbicides are natural alternatives to chemical herbicides that are derived from living organisms such as bacteria, fungi, and plants. Soil application involves applying herbicides to the soil to prevent weed emergence and growth. Foliar application targets weeds by spraying the herbicides directly on leaves. Post-harvest application is used to manage weeds after the crop has been harvested to prevent their regrowth.</p></p>
