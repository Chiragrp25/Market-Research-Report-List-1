<p><p>In the additive manufacturing market, various types of lasers are utilized for different purposes. He-Cd lasers use a mixture of helium and cadmium to generate a laser beam, which is suitable for processing materials like plastic and ceramics. Argon lasers produce a visible blue or green laser beam and are commonly used for engraving, welding, and cutting metals. Femtosecond lasers emit ultrashort pulses with extremely high peak power, enabling highly precise and minimal heat-affected zone processing. There are also other types of lasers used in additive manufacturing, each offering unique capabilities and applications.</p></p>
